# The six categories

Lynch's central tool: before analyzing a company, decide which *kind* it is. The category sets what you want from it, which numbers matter, the shape of the two-minute story, and — crucially — the signal that tells you to sell. Tag every watchlist/thesis/position note with the category (`#slow-grower`, `#stalwart`, `#fast-grower`, `#cyclical`, `#turnaround`, `#asset-play`). A company can shift categories over time; re-check it on review.

| Category | What you want | Numbers that matter most | Sell signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Slow grower** | A reliable, generous, well-covered dividend | Dividend history & payout ratio; debt; steady (if slow) earnings | Better use of the money; dividend at risk; fundamentals decay. Often: don't bother owning these. |
| **Stalwart** | A 30–50% gain then rotate; downside protection in a slump | P/E vs its own history; has it already run up? any catalyst accelerating growth? | P/E far above its normal range; the catalyst played out; growth stalls |
| **Fast grower** | The big winners — multibaggers — while growth lasts | Growth rate (and is it sustainable?); PEG; room to expand (how saturated?); debt | Growth slows / expansion saturates; P/E way ahead of the growth rate; the story's "act" is over |
| **Cyclical** | To catch the upswing; avoid holding into the downturn | Inventories; supply/demand & pricing; where in the cycle; capacity | Cycle peaks: inventories build, prices soften, new capacity floods in. Timing is everything. |
| **Turnaround** | A rebound from depressed levels, somewhat market-independent | Is the rescue plan working? cash vs debt (can it survive?); is the worst over? | The turnaround completed (it's now a normal company) or the plan visibly fails |
| **Asset play** | Hidden assets worth more than the price | Value of the assets (real estate, cash, brands, subsidiaries) vs market cap; debt against the assets | The market finally recognizes the assets; or debt erodes the asset cushion |

## Using categories in the workflow

- **In `assay`** — name the category first; it dictates the two-minute story you draft and the numbers you fetch. Don't apply a fast-grower lens to a cyclical.
- **In the thesis** — record the category and *which act you're in* (early growth vs. saturating; early turnaround vs. completed). 
- **On review (`pulse`/`brief`)** — re-tell the category's story and ask whether its specific sell signal has triggered. A fast grower whose growth is decelerating is the classic "the story changed" moment.
- **Holding discipline (Mayer/Lynch)** — for a fast grower still growing, the right move is usually to *sit tight*, not to take a quick profit. Selling a winner because it "went up" is pulling the flowers and watering the weeds. The sell trigger is the *story* changing, not the price moving.
